Why there’s room for Bitbucket in a GitHub World

Download this project as a .zip file Download this project as a tar.gz file

The Octocat is already out of the bag about GitHub. The 10k new users/day growth is accelerating (below graphic and link), the $100mn from Andreesen Horowitz likely makes it so they’ll never need outside funding again, and of course there’s the over 5mn repositories and counting – from jQuery to Rails to writing academic books, and so on.

So why don’t we all just jump on the GitHub bandwagon?

• Competition makes everyone better.

Want to put the breaks on the ferocious pace of DVCS disruption? Just have everyone raise their white flag to GitHub. Competition makes everyone continue to innovative and stay sharp. Especially as it’s possible GitHub gets acquired or IPOs. Good competition keeps everyone driving their best product to us users.

• Unlimited private repositories.

Let’s face it. There are times when you’re working on a new, cutting-edge project that you’re not ready to send into the world. Code security. Competitive advantage. You don’t want everyone to know what you’re working on always. Whatever your reason, Bitbucket provides FREE unlimited private repos. On the house. GRATIS.

• Double back-up.

Last September GitHub went down twice in two days. As GigaOm and the Twitter-sphere noted, that’s kind of a big deal – halting any number of 5mn projects is A LOT of projects – rendering any DVCS benefits irrevelant. Given Bitbucket’s cost-effective pricing, and the new features that make syncing between the two easier than ever, doubling back-up may even be a must do these days.

• Preference for the BB user interface.

Part of this can come down to your own personal “style” and/or how enamored you are with Octocats. Yes they're cute, however, some people simply find Bitbucket’s color scheme and UI cleaner and easier on the eye, especially if you’re already spending countless hours in front of a screen, like so many of us are. Ultimately, UI is an area where both services likely need to continue to improve. That said, if you ‘can dig’ BB’s schema, and integration with the below, you may have found your match.

• Integration with JIRA and other great ‘DevOps’ tools

In a world where time is of the essence and dev workflow “zen” paramount, this final point shouldn’t be understated. Sure, Jira also has good integration with GH. However, additional seamless integration with the SourceTree, Bamboo’s continuous integration, HipChat, and a steady stream of helpful content and tutorials might push more of us into the Bitbucket camp.

...or, at the very least demonstrates why there should be a place for Bitbucket in the emerging DVCS revolution.
